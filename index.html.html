Who We Are

Klub izgrađen na tradiciji A Club Built on Tradition

Rotari klub Banja Luka osnovan je 28. januara 1934. godine, a čarterovan 18. maja sljedeće godine. Osnivač i prvi predsjednik kluba bio je Artur Burda (1894–1984), ugledni građanin Banje Luke, privrednik i počasni poljski konzul. Klub je aktivno djelovao sve do pred Drugi svjetski rat, koji je 1941. godine zahvatio i naše krajeve. Rotary Club Banja Luka was founded on January 28, 1934, and chartered on May 18 of the following year. The founder and first president was Artur Burda (1894–1984), a distinguished citizen of Banja Luka, businessman and honorary Polish consul. The club was active until the onset of World War II, which reached our region in 1941.

Posljednji predsjednik kluba u tom periodu bio je poznati slikar, etnolog i direktor Muzeja Spiridon Špiro Bocarić (1876–1941). Nakon više od pola vijeka, ideja rotarijanstva u Banjoj Luci ponovo je zaživjela krajem XX vijeka zahvaljujući Dr Momčilu Biukoviću i grupi entuzijasta. The last president during that period was the renowned painter, ethnologist and museum director Spiridon Špiro Bocarić (1876–1941). After more than half a century, the idea of Rotarianism in Banja Luka was revived at the end of the 20th century thanks to Dr. Momčilo Biuković and a group of enthusiasts.

Danas je Rotari klub Banja Luka aktivan i u zajednici prepoznat klub po promociji izvrsnosti i po pružanju pomoći onima kojima je potrebna. Today, Rotary Club Banja Luka is an active and community-recognized club known for promoting excellence and helping those in need.

Povelja Spiridon Špiro Bocarić

The Spiridon Špiro Bocarić Award

Povelja nosi ime uglednog slikara, etnologa i posljednjeg predsjednika prvobitnog Rotari kluba Banja Luka, Spiridona Špire Bocarića (1876–1941). Dodjeljuje se istaknutim pojedincima koji su dali izuzetan doprinos zajednici u oblastima nauke i kulture.

The award bears the name of the renowned painter, ethnologist and last president of the original Rotary Club Banja Luka, Spiridon Špiro Bocarić (1876–1941). It is awarded to outstanding individuals who have made exceptional contributions to the community in the fields of science and culture.
